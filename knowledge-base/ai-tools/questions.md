# Questions Generator

Create interview-ready questions that podcast hosts and interviewers can ask you.

## Overview

The Questions Generator helps you create thoughtful questions that:
- Make podcast hosts' jobs easier
- Ensure great interviews
- Showcase your best insights
- Guide conversations productively

## Why Prepare Questions?

Pre-prepared questions help:
- **Hosts** - Less prep work, better interviews
- **You** - Share your best material consistently
- **Audience** - Get more value from the conversation
- **Everyone** - Avoid awkward silences and tangents

## Types of Questions

### Opener Questions
Get the conversation started:
- "What's your story? How did you get into this work?"
- "What's the biggest myth about [your field]?"
- "What's something you wish you knew earlier in your career?"

### Expertise Questions
Showcase your knowledge:
- "Walk us through your [Framework Name]..."
- "What's the first thing you look at when working with a new client?"
- "What separates good [practitioners] from great ones?"

### Story Questions
Prompt your signature stories:
- "Can you share an example of a client transformation?"
- "What was a major turning point in your career?"
- "What's a mistake you made that taught you something valuable?"

### Insight Questions
Share unique perspectives:
- "What's a contrarian view you hold about [industry]?"
- "What trend do you see that most people are missing?"
- "What's the future of [topic]?"

### Action Questions
Give the audience something to do:
- "What's one thing listeners can do today to [outcome]?"
- "What's the first step someone should take if they're struggling with [problem]?"
- "What resources would you recommend?"

## How to Use

### Step 1: Define Your Goals

What do you want listeners to:
- Learn?
- Feel?
- Do?

### Step 2: Generate Questions

1. Enter your topics and key talking points
2. Click **Generate Questions**
3. Review and select the best ones
4. Organize by category

### Step 3: Prepare Answers

For each question, outline:
- Your key points
- A relevant story
- A memorable soundbite
- A call to action

## Question Formulas

### The "What If" Question
> "What would happen if [audience] started [action]?"
*Lets you share results and possibilities.*

### The "Biggest Mistake" Question
> "What's the biggest mistake you see [audience] making?"
*Positions you as an expert who can help.*

### The "If Only" Question
> "What do you wish [audience] knew about [topic]?"
*Lets you share insider knowledge.*

### The "Walk Us Through" Question
> "Can you walk us through your process for [outcome]?"
*Showcases your methodology.*

### The "Real Talk" Question
> "What's something that's hard to hear but [audience] needs to know?"
*Shows your willingness to be honest.*

## Example Question Sets

### For a Business Consultant
1. "What made you leave corporate to start consulting?"
2. "What's the #1 mistake you see growing businesses make?"
3. "Walk us through your Revenue Acceleration System."
4. "Can you share a client success story?"
5. "What's one thing a business owner can do this week to grow?"

### For a Coach
1. "What's your coaching philosophy?"
2. "How do you know if someone is really ready for change?"
3. "What's a transformation you witnessed that still amazes you?"
4. "What's the hardest truth you have to tell clients?"
5. "Where can listeners learn more about working with you?"

### For an Author
1. "What inspired you to write this book?"
2. "What was the most surprising thing you learned while writing?"
3. "Who is this book really for?"
4. "What do you hope readers take away?"
5. "What's next for you after this book?"

## Organizing Your Questions

### Create a Menu
Offer hosts options:
- 5-7 "choose from these" questions
- Organized by topic or theme
- Marked with difficulty/depth level

### Include Notes
Add context for hosts:
- "Ask this if we have time for a story"
- "Great for audiences new to this topic"
- "This one usually gets good engagement"

### Have a Flow
Suggest an order that builds:
1. Personal/background opener
2. Problem/pain point
3. Your approach/framework
4. Success story
5. Actionable advice
6. Where to learn more

## Tips for Great Questions

### Avoid Yes/No Questions
**Bad:** "Is mindset important?"
**Good:** "How does mindset affect business success?"

### Build In Stories
**Bad:** "What's your framework?"
**Good:** "Can you share how you discovered this framework?"

### Create Soundbite Opportunities
**Bad:** "What advice do you have?"
**Good:** "What's the one thing you wish every entrepreneur knew?"

### End with Action
Always include a "what can listeners do today" question.
