# SEO Optimizer

Optimize your media kit and content for search engine discoverability.

## Overview

The SEO Optimizer helps you improve how your media kit and content appear in search results. Better SEO means:
- More organic traffic
- Higher visibility for your name
- Better positioning in searches
- Increased discovery by podcasters and media

## What is SEO for Personal Brands?

Search Engine Optimization for personal brands focuses on:
- **Name searches** - When people Google your name
- **Expertise searches** - When people search for topics you cover
- **Location searches** - "Expert in [topic] [city]"
- **Credential searches** - "[Topic] speaker," "[Topic] consultant"

## Key SEO Elements

### Page Title
The title that appears in browser tabs and search results.

**Format:**
> [Name] - [Title/Expertise] | [Brand/Company]

**Example:**
> Sarah Chen - Revenue Growth Expert | GrowthLab

### Meta Description
The snippet shown in search results (150-160 characters).

**Include:**
- Your name
- Key expertise
- Unique value
- Call to action

**Example:**
> Sarah Chen helps SaaS founders scale to $10M using the Revenue Acceleration System. Featured in Forbes. View media kit and book for interviews.

### Headings (H1, H2, H3)
Structure your content with keyword-rich headings:

```
H1: Sarah Chen - Revenue Growth Consultant
  H2: About Sarah
  H2: Speaking Topics
    H3: Scaling Service Businesses
    H3: Revenue Acceleration System
  H2: Media Appearances
```

### Image Alt Text
Describe images for accessibility and SEO:

**Profile photo:**
> "Sarah Chen, Revenue Growth Consultant and Speaker"

**Speaking photo:**
> "Sarah Chen speaking at SXSW 2024 about business scaling"

## Keyword Strategy

### Types of Keywords

**Name Keywords:**
- Your full name
- Common misspellings
- Name + profession

**Expertise Keywords:**
- Your main topics
- Industry terms
- Problem keywords (what you solve)

**Intent Keywords:**
- "[Topic] speaker"
- "[Topic] consultant"
- "[Topic] podcast guest"
- "Hire [topic] expert"

### Keyword Placement

Include keywords naturally in:
- Page title
- Meta description
- First paragraph
- Headings
- Image alt text
- URL slug

## Content Optimization Tips

### Write for Humans First
SEO matters, but readability matters more. Don't stuff keywords awkwardly.

### Use Your Name
Include your full name in key places:
- Page title
- First paragraph
- About section
- Image alt text

### Include Location
If relevant, mention your location:
> "Based in San Francisco, Sarah works with clients worldwide..."

### Link Internally
Link between pages on your site:
- Media kit → Blog posts
- Blog posts → Services
- All pages → Contact

### Get External Links
Quality links from other sites boost SEO:
- Guest posts with bio links
- Podcast show notes linking to you
- Media mentions
- Industry directory listings

## Media Kit SEO Checklist

### Basic SEO
- [ ] Descriptive page title with name
- [ ] Meta description under 160 characters
- [ ] Clean URL (yoursite.com/media-kit)
- [ ] H1 heading with your name
- [ ] Name in first paragraph

### Content SEO
- [ ] Keywords in headings
- [ ] Topics mentioned with keywords
- [ ] Alt text on all images
- [ ] Internal links to other pages
- [ ] External links to credible sources

### Technical SEO
- [ ] Mobile-friendly design
- [ ] Fast loading speed
- [ ] HTTPS enabled
- [ ] No broken links
- [ ] XML sitemap

### Regular Tasks
- Monthly: Review search performance
- Quarterly: Update keywords and content
- Annually: Major content refresh

## Common SEO Mistakes

### Keyword Stuffing
Don't repeat keywords unnaturally. Write naturally.

### Ignoring Meta Descriptions
These are your "ad copy" in search results. Write them intentionally.

### Missing Alt Text
Every image should have descriptive alt text.

### Slow Loading
Large images and complex scripts hurt rankings.

### No Mobile Optimization
Most searches are mobile. Ensure your site works well on phones.
