# Interview Prep

Prepare for podcast interviews and media appearances to deliver your best performance.

## Overview

The Interview Prep tool helps you get ready for podcast appearances and media interviews by:
- Researching the show and host
- Preparing answers to likely questions
- Identifying key messages to convey
- Organizing your best stories and soundbites

## Pre-Interview Checklist

### Research the Show
- [ ] Listen to 2-3 recent episodes
- [ ] Understand the audience
- [ ] Note the interview style
- [ ] Identify recurring themes

### Research the Host
- [ ] Review their background
- [ ] Check their social media
- [ ] Find common connections
- [ ] Note their interview approach

### Prepare Your Content
- [ ] Define 3 key messages
- [ ] Prepare relevant stories
- [ ] Ready soundbites/quotes
- [ ] Have your CTA ready

### Technical Setup
- [ ] Test audio/video equipment
- [ ] Find a quiet location
- [ ] Good lighting (for video)
- [ ] Backup plan if tech fails

## How to Use

### Step 1: Enter Show Details

Provide:
- Show name and URL
- Host name
- Interview topic (if known)
- Episode format (length, style)

### Step 2: Generate Prep Materials

1. Click **Generate Prep**
2. Review likely questions
3. Prepare your answers
4. Note key messages

### Step 3: Practice

- Record yourself answering questions
- Time your responses (aim for 2-3 minutes max)
- Practice transitioning to your key messages

## Key Messages Framework

### Identify 3 Key Messages

Whatever questions you're asked, you want to convey:
1. **Your unique insight** - What's your contrarian view?
2. **Your methodology** - What's your framework?
3. **Your call to action** - What should listeners do?

### Bridging Techniques

When a question doesn't directly relate to your messages:

**Bridge phrases:**
- "That's a great question, and it reminds me of..."
- "Yes, and what I've found is..."
- "Actually, the bigger issue is..."
- "That's exactly why I developed..."

## Preparing Stories

### Have These Ready

1. **Origin story** - How you got started (2 minutes)
2. **Transformation story** - A client success (2 minutes)
3. **Failure story** - A lesson learned (2 minutes)
4. **Signature story** - Your key teaching (2 minutes)

### Story Structure
Each story should follow:
- **Setup** - Context and characters
- **Conflict** - The challenge or problem
- **Resolution** - What happened
- **Lesson** - The takeaway

## Common Interview Questions

### Opening Questions
- "Tell us about yourself"
- "How did you get into this work?"
- "What's your background?"

### Expertise Questions
- "What's the biggest mistake you see?"
- "Walk us through your process"
- "What separates good from great?"

### Story Questions
- "Can you share an example?"
- "Tell us about a client who..."
- "What's a time when..."

### Insight Questions
- "What's a contrarian view you hold?"
- "What trend do you see coming?"
- "What do most people get wrong?"

### Tactical Questions
- "What should someone do first?"
- "How do you recommend..."
- "What's your advice for..."

### Closing Questions
- "Where can people find you?"
- "Any final thoughts?"
- "What's next for you?"

## Soundbite Preparation

### Craft Memorable Lines

Before the interview, prepare 3-5 soundbites:

**Examples:**
- "You can't outwork a broken system"
- "Scale your systems before you scale your team"
- "The best hire you make is the one you don't need"

### Practice Delivery

Soundbites should be:
- Under 10 words ideal
- Easy to quote/share
- Tied to your key messages
- Natural, not forced

## Technical Prep

### Audio
- Use a quality microphone
- Test audio levels
- Find a quiet space
- Use headphones

### Video (if applicable)
- Good lighting (face the light)
- Clean, professional background
- Camera at eye level
- Look at the camera, not the screen

### Connection
- Use wired internet if possible
- Close unnecessary applications
- Have a backup plan (phone hotspot)

## Day-of Checklist

### 1 Hour Before
- [ ] Review your prep materials
- [ ] Test equipment
- [ ] Water at your desk
- [ ] Phone on silent

### 5 Minutes Before
- [ ] Close extra browser tabs
- [ ] Deep breaths
- [ ] Smile and warm up voice
- [ ] Review your 3 key messages

### During the Interview
- Speak slightly slower than normal
- Pause before answering complex questions
- Tell stories, not just facts
- Bridge back to your key messages
- End with your call to action
