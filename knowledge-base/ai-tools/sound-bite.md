# Sound Bite Generator

Create memorable, quotable one-liners that stick in people's minds.

## Overview

A sound bite is a short, memorable phrase that:
- Captures a big idea in few words
- Is easy to quote and share
- Becomes associated with you
- Works across all platforms

## What Makes a Great Sound Bite?

### Characteristics
- **Short** - Usually under 15 words
- **Memorable** - Easy to recall
- **Quotable** - People want to share it
- **True** - Reflects genuine insight
- **Rhythmic** - Flows well when spoken

### Examples of Great Sound Bites
- "Move fast and break things" - Mark Zuckerberg
- "Be the change you wish to see" - Gandhi
- "Start with why" - Simon Sinek
- "Done is better than perfect" - Sheryl Sandberg

## Sound Bite Formulas

### The Contrast
> "It's not about working harder, it's about working smarter."

### The Unexpected Pairing
> "Strategy is just glorified common sense."

### The Reversal
> "The best way to predict the future is to create it."

### The Rule
> "When in doubt, cut it out."

### The Definition
> "Leadership is making good decisions when no one is watching."

### The Comparison
> "Culture eats strategy for breakfast."

### The Challenge
> "If you're not embarrassed by v1, you launched too late."

## Where to Use Sound Bites

### Interviews
End key points with a memorable phrase:
> "So ultimately, what I've learned is: systems before scale."

### Social Media
Stand-alone posts that get shared:
> "Hot take: The best marketing strategy is a great product."

### Presentations
Repeatable phrases that anchor your message:
> "Remember: done is better than perfect."

### Email Signatures
Below your name:
> "Helping founders build businesses that work without them."

### Website Headers
Homepage or about page:
> "Scaling service businesses to $10M and beyond."

## Tips for Memorable Sound Bites

### Keep It Short
If it can't fit in a tweet, it's too long.

### Use Rhythm
Patterns make things memorable:
- "Know your worth. Then add tax."
- "Less talk, more action."

### Be Specific
**Generic:** "Work hard and you'll succeed"
**Specific:** "Your first million is the hardest; your first ten thousand is the most important"

### Test Aloud
If it doesn't sound good spoken, revise it.

### Own It
Use your best sound bites consistently until they become associated with you.

## Common Mistakes

### Too Clever
Don't sacrifice clarity for cleverness.

### Too Long
If you have to explain it, it's not a sound bite.

### Too Generic
"Work hard, stay humble" could be anyone's line.

### Forcing It
Let sound bites emerge naturally from your ideas.
